CONTROLLED DOCUMENT: HOT WORK PERMIT PROCEDURE
Document Number: WMS-HSE-019
Version: 1.4
Approved: General Manager Operations
Review Date: November 2026

1. PURPOSE
This procedure governs the issue, use, and closure of Hot Work Permits for any work that produces sparks, flames, or heat sufficient to ignite flammable materials at Wirra Mine Site.

2. WHAT CONSTITUTES HOT WORK
Hot work includes but is not limited to welding, cutting, and grinding, use of open flames or heat guns, and any work creating sparks within 10 metres of a flammable or combustible material.

3. PERMIT REQUIREMENTS
3.1 A Hot Work Permit must be issued before any hot work commences.
3.2 Hot Work Permits are issued by the Site Safety Officer or delegate.
3.3 Permits are valid for a single shift and must be renewed for work continuing into the next shift.
3.4 A permit cannot be issued for work within a confined space without a simultaneous Confined Space Entry Permit.

4. PRE-WORK REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Remove or shield all flammable materials within 10 metres of the work area.
4.2 Test the atmosphere for flammable gases using a calibrated gas detector.
4.3 Ensure a fire extinguisher of minimum 9kg dry powder is within 5 metres of the work area.
4.4 Designate a fire watch person who is not engaged in the hot work itself.

5. FIRE WATCH REQUIREMENTS
5.1 A fire watch person must monitor the work area during all hot work.
5.2 The fire watch continues for a minimum of 30 minutes after hot work is completed.
5.3 The fire watch person must have access to a fire extinguisher and be trained in its use.
5.4 If a fire is detected, the fire watch person must raise the alarm and attempt suppression only if safe to do so.

6. PERMIT CLOSURE
6.1 The fire watch person must sign the permit closure after completing the 30-minute post-work watch.
6.2 Closed permits must be returned to the Site Safety Officer and retained for 12 months.
6.3 Any fire incident or near-miss during hot work must be reported on the incident management system within 24 hours.
