WIRRA MINE SITE
CONTROLLED DOCUMENT: ENVIRONMENTAL PERMIT CONDITIONS SUMMARY
Document Number: WMS-ENV-003
Version: 5.0
Approved: Environmental Manager
Review Date: January 2027

NOTE: This is a summary of key conditions from the Wirra Mine Site Environmental Licence, Licence Number WA-ENV-00412. The full licence document is the authoritative reference.

1. WATER MANAGEMENT
1.1 Discharge of site water to any natural watercourse is prohibited without prior written approval from the Department of Environment.
1.2 Turbidity of any water discharged from the site must not exceed 50 NTU at the point of discharge.
1.3 A monthly water balance report must be submitted to the Department of Environment by the 15th of each month.
1.4 All water storage dams must be inspected weekly. Inspection records must be retained for five years.

2. DUST MANAGEMENT
2.1 Haul roads must be watered at intervals not exceeding four hours during active operations.
2.2 Wind erosion controls such as revegetation or stabilisation must be applied to all inactive disturbed areas within 60 days of last disturbance.
2.3 Dustfall monitoring must be conducted at four perimeter locations in accordance with the approved Dust Management Plan.

3. NOISE
3.1 Operational noise at the nearest sensitive receptor must not exceed 45 dB(A) LAeq between 7:00am and 10:00pm.
3.2 Operational noise must not exceed 35 dB(A) LAeq between 10:00pm and 7:00am.
3.3 Blasting must not occur outside the hours of 9:00am to 5:00pm Monday to Saturday. Blasting is prohibited on Sundays and public holidays.

4. WASTE
4.1 Hydrocarbon waste including oils and fuels must be stored in bunded areas with capacity of 110 percent of the largest container.
4.2 Hazardous waste must not be disposed of on site and must be transported by a licensed waste contractor.
4.3 A waste register must be maintained and made available to the regulator upon request.

5. REPORTING AND INCIDENTS
5.1 Any environmental incident must be reported to the Department of Environment within 24 hours of becoming aware of the incident.
5.2 An annual Environmental Performance Report must be submitted to the Department of Environment by 31 March each year.
5.3 Licence conditions must be reviewed against performance quarterly by the Environmental Manager.
