WIRRA MINE SITE
CONTROLLED DOCUMENT: ISOLATION PROCEDURE
Document Number: WMS-HSE-004
Version: 3.2
Approved: General Manager Operations
Review Date: March 2027

1. PURPOSE
This procedure defines the requirements for isolating energy sources before maintenance or inspection work commences on plant and equipment at Wirra Mine Site.

2. SCOPE
Applies to all personnel performing or supervising work on electrical, mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic, chemical, or thermal energy sources.

3. ISOLATION STEPS
3.1 Identify all energy sources associated with the equipment.
3.2 Notify the Control Room Supervisor before commencing isolation.
3.3 Isolate each energy source at its primary isolation point.
3.4 Apply a personal danger tag and lock to each isolation point.
3.5 Test for zero energy by attempting to start the equipment and testing for voltage at terminals.
3.6 Record the isolation in the Isolation Register.

4. LOCK AND TAG REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Each worker must apply their own personal danger tag and lock.
4.2 Danger tags must include the worker name, date, and contact number.
4.3 Tags and locks must not be removed by anyone other than the person who applied them.
4.4 In the event of a shift handover, isolation must be formally transferred to the incoming worker.

5. ELECTRICAL ISOLATION
5.1 Electrical isolation must be performed by a licensed electrician.
5.2 Isolation points include main circuit breakers, motor starters, and VSD panels.
5.3 After isolation, test with an approved voltage tester at the work point.
5.4 For high voltage equipment above 1000V, a Permit to Work is required in addition to isolation.

6. MECHANICAL ISOLATION
6.1 Mechanical isolation includes locking out hydraulic and pneumatic systems.
6.2 Residual pressure must be bled to zero before work commences.
6.3 Gravity-loaded equipment such as conveyors and bins must be blocked or pinned.
6.4 Stored energy in springs, counterweights, and capacitors must be dissipated.

7. REMOVAL OF ISOLATION
7.1 Confirm all personnel are clear of the work area before removing isolation.
7.2 Remove all tools and foreign materials from the equipment.
7.3 Remove tags and locks in reverse order of application.
7.4 Notify the Control Room Supervisor that isolation has been removed.
7.5 Record removal in the Isolation Register.

8. EMERGENCY OVERRIDE
Emergency override of an isolation is only permitted by the Site Manager with written authorisation and must be recorded in the Isolation Register immediately.
